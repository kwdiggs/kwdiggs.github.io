From 2018 - 2020, I worked for CTSI-Global. I did a lot of work with web forms in Angular. Hydration, custom validation, etc. I also maintained/migrated multiple Angular web applications and trained an offshore dev in Chanai India.


After being hired, I delayed my start by 1 month to gain international experience in Japan. This image is me at "Tower of the Sun" statue in Osaka.

I also married my wife around this time. :)

Mind Over Data, LLC

At this shop, I helped develop inventory software for client AOC Resins. I worked with a senior dev, and it was here I learned Angular.

"No third-party dependecies" was a requirement for this job, so I created custom UI elements such as a ComboBox with autocomplete and also sync/async (API calls) option generation. It was faster than other UI library solutions because I took a stateful, iterative approach instead of the simpler functional route.

I also picked up some Razor templating skills (.NET) to create printer styles for custom reports (Yes, these styles were tedious, and I went through quite a bit of printer paper).

It was also during this time I accumulated some badges on StackOverflow for my Angular contributions.

Musistic, Inc.

During my senior year at university, I took on a developer-intern role at Musistic, Inc. Here, I worked in PHP's Yii framework for the main site, and WordPress for a subdomain. I had the opportunity to create both user-to-user messaging and notification systems, including the database design and the UI.

I also created a custom WordPress plugin. It was an audio file uploader that used ffmpeg for standardized encoding for the audio files that would be used in user profile pages. Also, a proof-of-concept mobile app for the Android platform at the request of my technical lead.

This photo is when our team represented the company at NAMM conf in Nashville.
